How to tell the REO story to an asset manager, bank, or servicer audience
▼
"An REO asset with a junior lien, an HOA delinquency, a valuation gap, and an unknown occupant. The AI builds the remediation path and tells the asset manager which problem to solve first."
REO portfolios are complex because every asset has a unique combination of title, occupancy, valuation, and legal issues — and the sequence in which you resolve them determines your timeline and your net recovery. Getting that sequence wrong adds months and tens of thousands in carrying costs.

The Harlow Ave asset in Phoenix has four concurrent issues: an unknown occupancy status (eviction pending), a $22k junior lien from an unpaid contractor, an HOA delinquency ($4,200), and a $23k BPO-to-appraisal valuation gap. Each issue has a different owner, a different timeline, and a different cost if left unresolved.

TSM's 7-tool remediation panel doesn't just list the issues. It sequences them by dependency: clear title first (junior lien), then confirm occupancy (eviction), then order a refreshed BPO, then resolve HOA delinquency before listing. Wrong sequence means you're negotiating a sale with a clouded title.
