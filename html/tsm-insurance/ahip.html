Medicare
Program Structure
Medicare is a federal health insurance program primarily for individuals 65 and older, as well as certain younger people with disabilities or End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD).
  • Part A — Hospital Insurance: inpatient care, skilled nursing, hospice, home health
  • Part B — Medical Insurance: outpatient care, preventive services, durable medical equipment
  • Part C — Medicare Advantage: bundled A+B (and usually D) through private plans
  • Part D — Prescription Drug Coverage: offered through private insurers
Eligibility Requirements
  • Age 65+ and a U.S. citizen or permanent legal resident (5+ years)
  • Under 65 with a qualifying disability after 24 months of SSDI
  • Any age with ESRD or ALS (Lou Gehrig's disease)
★Part A is premium-free for most beneficiaries who worked 40+ quarters (10 years) and paid Medicare taxes.
Enrollment Periods
  • IEP (Initial Enrollment Period) — 7-month window around 65th birthday (3 before, month of, 3 after)
  • GEP (General Enrollment Period) — Jan 1–Mar 31 each year; coverage begins July 1
  • SEP (Special Enrollment Period) — triggered by qualifying life events (loss of employer coverage, moving, etc.)
  • AEP (Annual Enrollment Period) — Oct 15–Dec 7; change MA or PDP plans
  • OEP (Open Enrollment Period) — Jan 1–Mar 31; switch MA plans once
★Late enrollment penalties apply for Part B (10% per 12-month period) and Part D (1% per month of delay).
Medicare Advantage (Part C)
MA plans must cover all Original Medicare benefits (except hospice). Common plan types:
  • HMO — must use network providers; referrals usually required
  • PPO — can use out-of-network providers at higher cost
  • PFFS — plan determines payment terms; providers may or may not accept
  • SNP — Special Needs Plans for chronic conditions, dual-eligible, or institutionalized
★MA plans have an annual out-of-pocket maximum — Original Medicare does not.

Fraud, Waste & Abuse
Definitions, examples, reporting obligations
FWA
Core Definitions
  • Fraud — Intentional deception or misrepresentation to obtain unauthorized benefit. Knowingly billing for services not rendered.
  • Waste — Overutilization of services or practices that result in unnecessary costs. Not typically intentional.
  • Abuse — Practices inconsistent with sound fiscal, business, or medical practices that result in unnecessary costs or reimbursements.
★Key distinction: Fraud requires INTENT. Waste and abuse may be inadvertent but still must be corrected and reported.
Common FWA Examples
  • Upcoding — billing for a higher-level service than provided
  • Unbundling — billing separately for services that should be billed together
  • Phantom billing — billing for services never rendered
  • Identity theft — using another person's Medicare ID to obtain benefits
  • Kickbacks — paying or receiving remuneration to induce referrals
  • Duplicate billing — submitting the same claim multiple times
Reporting Obligations
All agents and brokers are required to report suspected FWA. Reporting channels include:
  • CMS: 1-800-MEDICARE or medicare.gov
  • OIG Hotline: 1-800-HHS-TIPS
  • Plan sponsor's compliance hotline
  • State insurance department
★The False Claims Act allows whistleblowers (qui tam) to file suit on behalf of the government and receive a portion of recovered funds.
Penalties & Consequences
  • Civil monetary penalties up to $100,000 per violation
  • Exclusion from Medicare/Medicaid programs
  • Criminal prosecution for intentional fraud
  • License revocation by state insurance department
  • False Claims Act: up to 3x the amount of damages plus penalties per false claim

General Compliance
Laws, regulations, and agent obligations
Compliance
Key Laws & Regulations
  • Social Security Act — establishes Medicare program authority
  • HIPAA — protects beneficiary health information privacy
  • False Claims Act (FCA) — civil/criminal liability for false Medicare claims
  • Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS) — prohibits remuneration for referrals
  • Stark Law — physician self-referral prohibitions
  • HITECH Act — extends HIPAA protections to electronic records
Agent Obligations
  • Complete annual AHIP training and plan-specific certification
  • Maintain state insurance license in good standing
  • Follow CMS marketing guidelines at all times
  • Report FWA when discovered
  • Protect beneficiary PHI under HIPAA
  • Never accept gifts or payments from beneficiaries
★Agents must be appointed by each plan they sell. Selling without appointment is a compliance violation.
Compliance Program Elements
CMS requires MA and PDP sponsors to have compliance programs with 7 core elements:
  • Written policies and procedures
  • Compliance officer and committee
  • Effective training and education
  • Effective lines of communication
  • Well-publicized disciplinary standards
  • Effective system for routine monitoring/auditing
  • Procedures for responding to detected offenses

CMS Marketing Rules
Permitted activities, prohibited practices, scope of appointment
CMS Rules
Scope of Appointment (SOA)
The SOA documents what products a beneficiary agrees to discuss before a sales meeting.
  • Must be obtained 48 hours before the appointment (except walk-ins and beneficiary-initiated same-day requests)
  • Agent cannot discuss products outside the agreed scope
  • Must be retained for 10 years
  • Can be obtained via phone, in person, electronically, or by mail
★The 48-hour rule does NOT apply if the beneficiary initiates the contact and requests an immediate appointment.
Prohibited Marketing Activities
  • Cold calling (unsolicited door-to-door or phone contact)
  • Marketing at educational events
  • Offering gifts worth more than $15 (cash equivalent limit)
  • Conducting sales activities in healthcare settings (exam rooms, waiting areas)
  • Misleading or confusing beneficiaries about plan benefits
  • Using the Medicare name/logo in a misleading way
  • Cross-selling non-healthcare products during enrollment
Permitted Marketing Activities
  • Sending unsolicited direct mail
  • Placing ads in newspapers, TV, radio, internet
  • Staffing tables at community events (no approaching or pressuring)
  • Responding to beneficiary-initiated calls
  • Hosting sales/marketing events (with proper notice and no meal inducements)
★Educational events must remain purely educational — no plan-specific enrollment materials or sales pitches allowed.
Agent Compensation Rules
  • CMS sets maximum compensation limits for MA and PDP enrollments annually
  • Compensation must be the same regardless of which plan the beneficiary selects
  • Agents cannot receive higher compensation for switching a beneficiary unnecessarily
  • Renewal commissions are lower than initial enrollment commissions
