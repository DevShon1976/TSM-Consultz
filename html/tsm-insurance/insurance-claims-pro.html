DME Audit Failure — Incomplete CMN

DME-0441 · Oxygen Concentrator · Medicare Part B · 48HR DEADLINE
HARD
Oxygen concentrator claim denied — incomplete Certificate of Medical Necessity (CMN). Medicare requires specific diagnostic codes and physician attestation. Patient oxygen-dependent. Equipment already delivered. $3,200 at risk.
COMPLIANCE BILLING LEGAL INSURANCE

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
COMPLIANCE: Pull CMN template — identify missing fields (ICD-10, physician signature, qualifying diagnosis)
2
BILLING: Verify HCPCS code E1390 and modifier RT — check LCD for coverage criteria
3
INSURANCE: Contact Medicare — confirm resubmission window and corrected claim process
4
LEGAL: Review ABN requirement — confirm patient liability if claim ultimately denied
5
POC PORTAL: Generate corrected CMN package + Medicare appeal letter
⚡ INSURANCE TIPDME claims require valid CMN with ICD-10 supporting medical necessity. HCPCS E1390 = stationary oxygen. Medicare LCD requires PaO2 ≤55 mmHg or SpO2 ≤88% documented. Corrected claims must be submitted within the timely filing window.

TRACK 2 · PRIOR AUTH · COMMERCIAL PAYER

Prior Auth Denied — Step Therapy Not Met

PA-REQ-0882 · Biologic Medication · Aetna · PEER-TO-PEER REQUIRED
HARD
Prior authorization denied for biologic medication (Humira, J0135). Aetna requires step therapy — patient must fail 2 conventional DMARDs first. Physician believes step therapy contraindicated due to comorbidities. Peer-to-peer review requested. $8,400/month at risk.
PHARMACY MEDICAL COMPLIANCE LEGAL

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
MEDICAL: Document medical necessity — why step therapy is contraindicated (comorbidity evidence)
2
PHARMACY: File step therapy exception request — attach clinical documentation
3
COMPLIANCE: Review state step therapy law — 29 states have step therapy reform laws
4
LEGAL: Schedule peer-to-peer with Aetna medical director — prepare clinical summary
5
POC PORTAL: Generate step therapy exception appeal + peer-to-peer brief for physician
⚡ INSURANCE TIPStep therapy exceptions are granted when: (1) required drugs are contraindicated, (2) patient previously failed the required drugs, (3) clinical urgency prevents delay. Always document the specific contraindication, not just "not appropriate."

CO-197 Denial — Prior Auth Missing on Filed Claim

CLM-1192 · CPT 27447 · UnitedHealthcare · 30-DAY APPEAL WINDOW
HARD
Claim denied CO-197 — prior authorization not obtained. Total knee replacement (CPT 27447) was authorized but authorization number was omitted from claim header. Auth IS on file. This is a correctable administrative error. $28,000 at risk. Appeal deadline in 30 days.
BILLING INSURANCE COMPLIANCE OPERATIONS

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
BILLING: Pull original claim — locate auth number in payer portal — confirm auth was active on date of service
2
INSURANCE: Submit corrected claim (FL 63) with authorization number in the appropriate field

COMPLIANCE: Audit workflow to identify why auth number was not included at time of submission
4
OPERATIONS: Update pre-auth checklist — add auth number verification step before claim release
5
POC PORTAL: Generate corrected claim appeal + workflow correction memo for billing team
⚡ INSURANCE TIPCO-197 denials where auth EXISTS are highly recoverable via corrected claim submission — no formal appeal needed. Key: submit as corrected claim (not appeal) within the original timely filing window. Document the auth number was in the payer system on DOS.

TRACK 4 · ELIGIBILITY · PATIENT ACCESS · REGISTRATION

Eligibility Failure — Coverage Terminated Mid-Service

ELG-0033 · BCBS · Coverage Lapse · Retroactive Termination · PATIENT LIABILITY
MEDIUM
271 eligibility check showed active coverage at registration. BCBS retroactively terminated coverage 2 days before date of service due to non-payment of premium. Services rendered were $4,100. Patient has 30-day grace period under ACA. Registration missed the lapse flag.
INSURANCE COMPLIANCE BILLING OPERATIONS

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
INSURANCE: Confirm ACA 30-day grace period — verify whether DOS falls within grace period window
2
COMPLIANCE: Review retroactive termination policy — determine if BCBS followed proper notification protocol
3
BILLING: Bill patient for services if grace period expired — attach detailed financial counseling note
4
OPERATIONS: Update eligibility check protocol — add coverage lapse flag to registration workflow
5
POC PORTAL: Generate patient financial responsibility letter + ACA grace period appeal to BCBS
⚡ INSURANCE TIPUnder ACA, marketplace plans have a 3-month grace period for premium non-payment (1 month for employer plans). During grace period months 2-3, payers can pend — not deny — claims. If coverage reinstated, claims must be paid. If not, only month 1 of grace period must be paid by payer.

TRACK 5 · COB · MEDICARE SECONDARY · MSPQ

COB Error — Medicare Billed Primary When Secondary

COB-7721 · Medicare + BCBS · MSPQ Not Completed · $12,400 OVERBILLING RISK
HARD
Medicare was billed primary but patient has active employer group health plan (EGHP) through BCBS — making Medicare secondary. Medicare Secondary Payer Questionnaire (MSPQ) was not completed at registration. Medicare paid $12,400 as primary. This creates a CMS overpayment liability that must be reported and refunded.
COMPLIANCE BILLING INSURANCE LEGAL

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
COMPLIANCE: Confirm MSP violation — determine if mandatory reporting applies under Section 111 reporting
2
BILLING: Refund Medicare overpayment — void Medicare claim — resubmit BCBS as primary
3
INSURANCE: Submit corrected claim to BCBS as primary — include Medicare as secondary in COB field
4
LEGAL: Assess False Claims Act exposure — document self-disclosure and corrective action
5
POC PORTAL: Generate CMS voluntary refund letter + corrective action plan + MSPQ protocol update
⚡ INSURANCE TIPMedicare Secondary Payer rules are among the most enforced in healthcare. When an active EGHP covers a Medicare beneficiary under 65, EGHP is primary. Failure to refund MSP overpayments within 60 days of identification creates False Claims Act liability. Always complete MSPQ at registration.

TRACK 6 · MEDICARE ADVANTAGE · RISK ADJUSTMENT · AUDIT

MA Risk Adjustment Audit — HCC Coding Gaps

RADV-2026 · 14 HCC Codes Under-Documented · CMS Audit · $48K REVENUE IMPACT
HARD
CMS Risk Adjustment Data Validation (RADV) audit selected 30 beneficiaries. Pre-audit analysis found 14 HCC conditions documented in clinical notes but NOT coded on claims. Under-documentation reduces risk scores and capitation payments. Estimated revenue impact $48,000 annually. Prospective coding education needed.
MEDICAL COMPLIANCE BILLING FINANCIAL LEGAL

MISSION OBJECTIVES

1
MEDICAL: Conduct retrospective chart review — confirm 14 HCCs are clinically supported in documentation
2
BILLING: Submit addendum claims with missing HCC codes — verify each has current-year face-to-face documentation
3
COMPLIANCE: Prepare RADV audit response — organize medical records per CMS submission requirements
4
FINANCIAL: Model revenue impact of corrected risk scores — present to CFO with prospective capture plan
5
POC PORTAL: Generate HCC coding gap analysis report + physician education memo + CFO revenue impact brief
⚡ INSURANCE TIPHCC (Hierarchical Condition Categories) drive MA capitation payments. Each chronic condition must be documented and coded every year — prior year coding does not carry forward. Common gaps: CKD staging, diabetic complications, BMI, COPD severity. Each uncaptured HCC = $800-$3,000 in annual capitation loss.
