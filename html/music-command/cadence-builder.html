SYLLABLE TARGET
At 140 BPM, 8–11 syllables per bar is the sweet spot. Under 6 sounds sparse. Over 14 starts to sound rushed.
STRESSED SYLLABLES
Land your stressed syllables on beats 1 and 3. "I KNOW you AIN'T gon-na LEAVE me" — the caps land on the beat.
DOUBLES
After recording a verse, re-record the same bar slightly behind or ahead. Doubles give your voice width and authority.
INTERNAL RHYMES
Don't just rhyme at the end of bars. "I ride at NIGHT, the LIGHT is BRIGHT" — mid-bar rhymes add drive and energy.
PUNCHLINES
Set up in bar 1, land the punchline in bar 2. Give yourself one breath between. The pause is part of the delivery.
SPACE IS FLOW
Leaving empty beats isn't a mistake — it's phrasing. Rod Wave and Drake let beats breathe. Don't fill every slot.
