ADLIBS + BRIDGE
When & how to use them
▼
WHEN TO ADD ADLIBS
→ After a hard-hitting bar — let the adlib punctuate the moment (Yeah! / Talk to 'em!)
→ Under the hook — low in the mix, reactive, never competing
→ At beat 4 before a new section drop — creates anticipation
→ Outro only — let them stack and build energy for the fade
BRIDGE RULES
→ Only use a bridge if the song needs a gear shift — emotional contrast or revelation
→ Drop the BPM feel — less dense, more space, let the listener breathe
→ 8 bars max — it's a moment, not a third verse
→ End the bridge going UP in energy to set up the final hook explosion
ZAY TIP: Adlibs are seasoning — not the meal. Use them to flavor what's already fire.

DAW SESSION SETUP
Before you open your DAW
▼
SESSION PREP (BEFORE RECORDING)
→ Set your BPM before anything — every track snaps to the grid from bar 1
→ Label your tracks: Lead, Hook Double L, Hook Double R, Verse Ad, Hype Ad, 808, Snare, Sample
→ Drop markers at every section — INTRO, V1, PRE, HOOK, V2, BRIDGE, OUTRO
VOCAL CHAIN (ESSENTIALS)
→ High-pass filter at 80Hz on every vocal track — cuts mud
→ Compress lead: ratio 4:1, attack 10ms, release 40ms — keeps it punchy not squashed
→ De-ess at 6-8kHz if the esses are harsh
→ Reverb on a SEND — never directly on the vocal track
ARRANGEMENT TIMING
0:00Intro (4-8 bars) — instrumental or vocal snippet 0:15Hook — hit them early, establish the song 0:40Verse 1 — 16 bars, build the story 1:20Pre-Hook + Hook 1:45Verse 2 — go deeper, raise stakes 2:25Bridge (optional) — emotional shift 2:50Final Hook — stacked, doubled, full energy 3:20Outro — fade or cold stop
ZAY TIP: Build your session template ONCE and save it. Every song starts clean but structured.

HOOK WRITING FORMULA
What makes a hook stick
▼
THE 4 ELEMENTS OF A STICKY HOOK
01
REPETITION — Say the title or key phrase at least twice. Make it unavoidable.
02
MELODY MOVEMENT — The hook melody should move UP on the emotional word. Down to resolve.
03
CONTRAST — Hook energy should feel different from the verse. If verse is dense, hook breathes.
04
SINGABILITY — Can someone hum it after one listen? If not, simplify.
COMMON HOOK STRUCTURES
A-A-B-ASame line × 2, different line, return — classic and bulletproof
A-B-A-BAlternating — creates conversation/tension between two ideas
A-A-A-BTriple statement then twist — maximum impact on the final line
ZAY TIP: Write the hook FIRST. Then write verses that set it up perfectly.
