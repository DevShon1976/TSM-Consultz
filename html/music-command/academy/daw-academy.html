// BEGINNER PRODUCTION ROADMAP
01

Choose Your DAW

Start with GarageBand (free, Mac) or FL Studio (best for hip-hop/trap). Don't switch until you've made 10+ songs.

02

Learn the Interface

Spend 2 weeks just learning: how to load a sample, set tempo, arm a track, and record audio. Don't try to mix yet.

03

Find Your Beats

Use BeatStars or Airbit for licensed beats. For originals, FL Studio's default sounds are enough to start.

04

Record Your First Take

Plug in any USB mic, get quiet, and record. Don't obsess over quality — finish the song first.

05

Basic Mixing

Volume balance first. Then EQ (cut mud around 200-300Hz on vocals). Then add a little reverb. Stop there.

06

Export & Listen

Export as WAV at 44.1kHz / 24-bit. Play it on phone speakers, car, earbuds. Note what sounds off.

07

Repeat 20 Times

The first 20 songs are practice. Focus on finishing — not perfecting. Your 20th song will sound 10x better than your first.
