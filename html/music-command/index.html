CADENCE TOOLS GUIDE
How to use Tap Tempo, Cadence Guide & Speak Cues
▼
Cadence is the rhythmic flow of your vocal delivery — how your bars land on the beat. These tools help you feel the pocket before you record.
👆
TAP TEMPO
Tap the button in rhythm with your beat — like tapping your foot. After 3–4 taps it calculates your BPM. All producer notes sync to this tempo.
💡 Pull up your beat on Spotify, play it, and tap along to lock your session to the actual BPM.
🥁
PLAY CADENCE GUIDE
Plays a metronome click in your browser at your tapped BPM. Beat 1 = sharp punch, Beat 3 = accent, 2 & 4 = ghost. Use this while reading your lyrics to find your flow before recording.
🗣️
SPEAK CADENCE CUES
Browser text-to-speech reads ZAY's delivery instructions out loud — hands-free coaching while you rap. Click it, then immediately start reading your hook aloud.
Recommended Workflow
01Fill concept → Build Blueprint
02Generate Beat Prompt → paste into Suno/Udio → get your beat
03Paste beat link into Beat Player (🎵 button, bottom right)
04Tap Tempo along with your beat to lock BPM
05Play Cadence Guide → practice hook flow to the click
06Speak Cadence Cues while rapping — ZAY coaches hands-free
07Record. You know your pocket.
Note: Cadence tools use your browser's built-in audio — no plugins needed. On mobile, tap the screen first to unlock audio.

ADLIBS + BRIDGE
When & how to use them
▼
WHEN TO ADD ADLIBS
→After a hard-hitting bar — punctuate the moment (Yeah! / Talk to 'em!)
→Under the hook — low in the mix, reactive, never competing with the lead
→At beat 4 before a new section — creates anticipation for the drop
→Outro only — let them stack and build energy for the fade
BRIDGE RULES
→Only use a bridge if the song needs a gear shift — emotional contrast or revelation
→Drop the density — less notes, more space, let the listener breathe
→8 bars max — it's a moment, not a third verse
→End the bridge going UP in energy to set up the final hook explosion
ZAY TIP: Adlibs are seasoning — not the meal. Use them to flavor what's already fire.
🎚️
DAW SESSION SETUP
Before you open your DAW
▼
SESSION PREP
→Set your BPM before anything — every track snaps to the grid from bar 1
→Label tracks: Lead, Hook Double L/R, Verse Ad, Hype Ad, 808, Snare, Sample
→Drop markers at every section — INTRO, V1, PRE, HOOK, V2, BRIDGE, OUTRO
VOCAL CHAIN ESSENTIALS
→High-pass filter at 80Hz on every vocal — cuts mud
→Compress lead: 4:1 ratio, 10ms attack, 40ms release
→De-ess at 6–8kHz if esses are harsh
→Reverb on a SEND — never directly on the vocal track
ARRANGEMENT TIMELINE
0:00Intro (4–8 bars) 0:15Hook — hit them early 0:40Verse 1 — 16 bars, build the story 1:20Pre-Hook + Hook 1:45Verse 2 — go deeper 2:25Bridge (optional) — emotional shift 2:50Final Hook — stacked, doubled, full energy 3:20Outro — fade or cold stop
ZAY TIP: Build your session template ONCE and save it. Every song starts clean but structured.

HOOK WRITING FORMULA
What makes a hook stick
▼
THE 4 ELEMENTS OF A STICKY HOOK
01
REPETITION — Say the title or key phrase at least twice. Make it unavoidable.
02
MELODY MOVEMENT — Hook melody moves UP on the emotional word, DOWN to resolve.
03
CONTRAST — Hook energy differs from the verse. Dense verse → breathing hook.
04
SINGABILITY — Can someone hum it after one listen? If not, simplify.
COMMON HOOK STRUCTURES
A-A-B-A Same line × 2, different line, return — classic and bulletproof
A-B-A-B Alternating — creates conversation between two ideas
A-A-A-B Triple statement then twist — maximum impact on final line
ZAY TIP: Write the hook FIRST. Then write verses that set it up perfectly.
