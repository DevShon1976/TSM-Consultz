Recording Coach

Most home vocal takes fail on setup, not talent. Work through this checklist before you hit record — then ask the AI coach anything specific to your room and mic.

// PRE-RECORD CHECKLIST
Mic 6–8 inches from mouth, slightly off-axisReduces plosives (P/B pops) and sibilance without losing presence
Pop filter in placeIf you don't have one, a clean sock stretched over a wire hanger works
Room treated — soft surfaces nearbyClose curtains, hang a blanket, record in a closet full of clothes if needed
Fans, AC, and phone notifications offBackground hum is invisible live but ruins a mix later
Gain staged so peaks hit around -12dB to -6dBLoud enough to sound clean, far enough from 0dB to avoid clipping
Headphones on, beat in one ear or both at low volumePrevents bleed into the mic while keeping you in time and in key
Plan for 3+ takes per sectionComp the best lines from multiple takes rather than chasing one perfect run
Record adlibs and doubles separatelyDoubling the hook and panning it L/R is the cheapest way to sound "radio ready"
