TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › OVERVIEW
SECTION 00
What is TSM Career OS?
A real-time healthcare operations simulator that puts you inside the KPI dashboards, denial management crises, and strategic decisions that healthcare ops professionals face every day — with AI-powered analysis scored by Groq's llama-3.3-70b model.
PURPOSE

This tool is not a quiz or a passive tutorial. It simulates real work scenarios so you can practice the thinking, language, and decisions you'll need to demonstrate in interviews and on the job. Every output you generate can be converted into a resume bullet or interview story.

◆
Six role lenses — switch between Business Analyst, Revenue Cycle, Process Improvement, Implementation Spec, Solutions Engineer, and Customer Success to see how the same data is interpreted differently by each role.
◆
Six live scenarios — denial spikes, AR aging crises, RAC audits, timely filing deadlines, upcoding flags, and no-show revenue loss. Each resets the KPIs and triggers fresh AI analysis.
◆
AI-powered outputs — every analysis is generated live by Groq llama-3.3-70b through your Railway endpoint — not pre-written templates.
◆
Interview Mode — AI-scored answers using the OBSERVE → DIAGNOSE → IMPACT → SOLVE → REPORT framework.
◆
Portfolio export — every analysis you run can be turned into a copy-ready resume bullet or LinkedIn post.
TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › FIRST-TIME SETUP
SECTION 01
First-Time Setup — Connecting Groq
The app requires a live Groq API connection to generate AI analysis. Check the status pill in the top-right corner before running anything.
GROQ: CHECKING...
App is testing your Railway endpoint on load. Wait 2–3 seconds.
✓ GROQ: LIVE
You're connected. Railway endpoint responded. All AI features are active. No action needed.
⚠ GROQ: OFFLINE
Railway endpoint not responding. Follow the steps below to reconnect.
1
Verify your Railway environment variable
Open railway.com → your TSM project → Variables tab → confirm GROQ_API_KEY is set to a valid gsk_... key from console.groq.com.
2
For instant local testing — browser console fallback
Open browser DevTools (F12) → Console tab → paste: window.GROQ_API_KEY = "gsk_your_key_here" and press Enter. The app will immediately detect and use this key without a page reload.
3
Confirm connection
The top-right pill should change to ✓ GROQ: LIVE within a few seconds. The banner at the top of the left panel will also update to confirm.
IMPORTANT

The console fallback (window.GROQ_API_KEY) only lasts for the current browser session. For persistent access, set the variable in Railway and redeploy.

SECTION 02
Screen Anatomy
The app is divided into five zones. Understanding each zone helps you navigate without confusion.
◆ ZONE MAP — TSM CAREER OS
① TOP BAR
Logo + Status Pills. Shows your current role mode, training loop step (e.g. STEP 3 OF 8), and Groq connection status. These update automatically as you work.
② ROLE BAR
Role Lens switcher. Seven buttons — one per role. Click to switch your analytical perspective. The entire app re-orients: KPI focus, AI system prompt, interview questions, career language, and app toolkit all update.
③ LEFT PANEL
Main workspace. Contains the KPI grid, system events feed, analyst output tabs, and (in Interview Mode) the question bank and answer scorer. This is where all analysis is displayed.
④ RIGHT PANEL
Training Loop + Scenario Launcher. Shows your 8-step daily training progression, scenario buttons, quick-launch app links, and career language examples. Always visible alongside your main workspace.
⑤ BOTTOM BAR
AI Query Input. The command line at the very bottom. Type any custom question, then press Enter or click RUN ⏎. The placeholder text updates to reflect your active role.
ORIENTATION TIP

The left panel scrolls independently. If you don't see output after running a query, scroll up in the left panel — the KPI grid and events section sit above the output area.

TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › OUTPUT TABS
SECTION 05
Output Tabs — Reading Your Analysis
After running a query or scenario, the analyst output section shows multiple tabs. Each one surfaces a different dimension of the same analysis.
EXEC SUMMARY
The default view. A 150–200 word executive-level summary of current state, key metrics, and bottom-line assessment. Written in the voice of your active role. Use this as your LinkedIn post or interview talking point.
ROOT CAUSE
Primary, secondary, and tertiary root causes of the current KPI problem. Includes causal chain explanation (e.g. EHR template → auto-applies modifier → CO-4 denial pattern).
IMPACT
Full financial and operational exposure quantified in $, %, staff hours, and compliance risk. This is what you cite in interviews when asked "how did you quantify the problem?"
RECOMMENDATION
Prioritized action plan with 48hr / Week 1 / Month 1 / Month 3 timeframes. Named owners per action. Projected outcome in $ and %.
🛠 APP TOOLKIT
Two-part view. Left: role-specific app suite (your personal toolbox). Right: recommended solution stack for this specific scenario. Each app card shows how you'd use it and includes a potential interview question about it. Click any card to expand the detail.
HOW TO SOLVE
Step-by-step execution workflow. Each step tells you which app to open, what to review, and what you should find. Click OPEN APP ↗ to mark a step complete and simulate working through the workflow.
KPI FORECAST
Animated bar chart showing current KPIs vs projected post-remediation state. Includes denial rate, AR days, clean claim rate, revenue recovery, and appeal win rate projections. The ⬇ EXPORT EXECUTIVE SUMMARY button simulates handing this to leadership.
PORTFOLIO
Copy-ready resume bullet + LinkedIn post angle + interview STAR story. Directly usable in job applications. See Section 10 for full detail.
WORKFLOW SUGGESTION

For each scenario you run: start at EXEC SUMMARY → drill to ROOT CAUSE → check IMPACT for the dollar figure → go to RECOMMENDATION for your action plan. Then click HOW TO SOLVE to understand the execution steps, and KPI FORECAST to see the projected outcome. This mirrors exactly what you'd present in a real operations role or interview.

TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › TRAINING LOOP
SECTION 06
The Daily Training Loop
The right panel tracks your progress through an 8-step training sequence. The current active step is highlighted. Steps advance automatically as you use the app.
STEP 1
OBSERVE
Review the KPI grid. Identify what's abnormal — which number is red, which is trending wrong, what's the magnitude of the variance. This step activates on load.
STEP 2
ROLE LENS
Click a role button. Ask yourself: what does someone in this role focus on first? A BA looks at benchmark gaps; an RCM analyst looks at denial codes. This step advances when you switch roles.
STEP 3
AI ANALYSIS
Run a query or scenario. Read the output. Look for: 1 key insight, 1 identified problem, 1 recommendation. This step marks complete when Groq returns analysis.
STEP 4
CONSULTANT OUTPUT
Check the EXEC SUMMARY and RECOMMENDATION tabs. These show you how a consultant would present this. Practice re-reading them out loud as if presenting to a client. Advances when you open a tab.
STEP 5
IMPROVE THE SYSTEM
Type your own product improvement idea into the query bar. Prompt: "As a [role], what one feature would I add to this system to prevent this problem?" This builds product thinking alongside domain knowledge.
STEP 6
APP SELECTION QUIZ
A three-option multiple-choice question appears in the right panel. Which app do you open first for this type of problem? Click your answer. Correct answer unlocks Step 7.
STEP 7
EXECUTION PLAN QUIZ
A second question: once inside the app, what do you review first? Tests procedural knowledge, not just tool selection. Unlocks after Step 6 correct answer.
STEP 8
BUSINESS OUTCOME
The KPI outcomes panel appears showing exactly which metrics improve and by how much after the recommended workflow. This is your "result" for any STAR answer: denial rate drops, revenue recovers, AR normalizes.
LOOP RESET

The loop does not reset automatically between scenarios. To restart, refresh the page. The top-right pill shows your current step at all times (e.g. STEP 4 OF 8). Steps 6–8 only appear once per session — they're designed to be completed in sequence after running at least one scenario.

TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › INTERVIEW MODE
SECTION 08
Interview Mode — AI-Scored Practice
Interview Mode replaces the analyst output panel with a full interview drill experience. Your answers are scored live by Groq using the OBSERVE → DIAGNOSE → IMPACT → SOLVE → REPORT framework.
1
Activate Interview Mode
Click the 🎯 Interview Mode button in the Role Bar. The analyst output section hides and an interview panel appears below the events feed.
2
Choose a question from the question bank
The question bank at the top of the interview panel lists 5–6 questions. Questions tagged 🛠 APP have a direct connection to a specific app or tool you've built — use these for your strongest STAR stories. Click any question to load it.
3
Review the framework steps
Below the question, five framework steps appear (Situation → Task → Action → Result → Connect). These are your answer structure. Read them before writing.
4
Write your answer
Type in the answer textarea. Aim for 100–200 words. Use the framework steps as your guide. Be specific — name the metric, name the action, name the outcome in % or $.
5
Score, hint, or advance
Click SCORE MY ANSWER for AI feedback. Click GET HINT for a 3-bullet coaching hint without revealing the full answer. Click NEXT QUESTION › to advance to the next question in the bank.
OBSERVE
Set the scene
1–2 sentences max. What system or process? What metric was off? Avoid over-explaining context — interviewers want to hear what you did, not what the company was.
DIAGNOSE
Name the root cause
What was the actual cause — not just the symptom? Name the process failure, the data gap, or the system limitation. "Denial rate was high" is a symptom; "modifier -25 was auto-applied by EHR template" is a diagnosis.
IMPACT
Quantify everything
$ at risk, % gap, days delayed, claims affected, staff hours consumed. If you don't have a real number, use the simulator's data: "18.4% denial rate — 3.4pt above CMS threshold — $48K stalled."
SOLVE
Describe your action
What specifically did you do? Name the tool, the method, the timeline, and who you worked with. Vague verbs ("I helped improve...") lose points. Specific verbs ("I built a pre-submission edit rule that caught...") win them.
REPORT
State the result
Outcome in measurable terms. "Denial rate dropped from 18.4% to 12% in 60 days." Then connect: "This is the same analysis I would apply on Day 1 in this role."
CONNECT-TO-ROLE
Bridge to the job
The "C" in STAR+C. After your result, add one sentence that directly connects your experience to the role you're interviewing for. This is the difference between a good answer and a great one.
COMMON MISTAKE

Most users write their OBSERVE section too long and their SOLVE section too short. Interviewers already know the healthcare context — they want to hear what you did. Spend 70% of your word count on SOLVE and REPORT.

Your personal tool portfolio. 3 apps specific to your active role — the tools someone in that role actually uses. RECOMMENDED STACK
(Scenario-specific)
Best tools for this specific crisis. 4 app cards in a 2×2 grid — Financial Intelligence, Doc Analysis Engine, Financial Operations, FinOps Accounting. Click a card to highlight (select) it. Click WHY THIS APP? to see a structured rationale: Observation → Cause → Impact → Outcome.
HOW TO USE THIS IN AN INTERVIEW

When asked "what tools do you use in your current work?" — do not just list software names. Use the WHY THIS APP rationale structure: "I use Financial Intelligence when I see a denial spike because it lets me isolate the top denial code by payer within 10 minutes, which is the fastest path to root cause." That is a demonstrably better answer than "I use Excel and a clearinghouse portal."

TSM CAREER OS › USER GUIDE › FAQ
SECTION 11
Frequently Asked Questions
The AI output didn't appear. What happened?
First check the ⚠ GROQ: OFFLINE pill in the top right. If offline, follow the setup steps in Section 01. If the pill shows connected but output is blank, scroll up in the left panel — the output box sits below the KPI grid and events feed. Also check the error message that sometimes appears in red below the output area.
The KPI numbers didn't change when I ran a scenario.
Only certain KPIs update per scenario. For example, the denial spike scenario updates the denial rate (K1) and stalled revenue (K4), but not days in AR or clean claim rate. This is intentional — not every scenario affects every metric. Look at the events feed for what changed.
How do I reset the KPIs back to the default state?
Refresh the page (F5 or Cmd+R). The app reinitializes with the default KPI state: 18.4% denial rate, 52 days in AR, 94.2% clean claim rate, $48K stalled revenue, 68% appeal win rate, 1,256 claims/month.
Interview Mode questions aren't showing up.
Confirm you clicked the 🎯 Interview Mode button in the role bar (not just any other role button). The interview panel appears at the bottom of the left panel — you may need to scroll down past the events section to see it.
The training loop is stuck on Step 1 / Step 2.
Steps advance based on specific actions: Step 2 requires a role switch, Step 3 requires a query run, Step 4 requires clicking an output tab. If you land on Step 1, try switching roles first (advances to Step 2), then run a scenario (advances to Step 3).
Steps 6–8 aren't appearing in the right panel.
Steps 6–8 are quiz steps that become visible once you reach Step 6. They're always present in the right panel DOM — scroll down in the right panel past the visible training steps. The Step 6 quiz (App Selection) appears after you've completed Step 5.
Can I add my own scenarios or questions?
Not from within the app UI. However, the app's source code is in your GitHub repository (Torreyw94/tsm-shell). You can add scenarios to the SCENARIOS object and questions to each role's questions array in the script section. See the existing entries for the exact structure to follow.
The KPI Forecast bars aren't animating.
The bars animate with a CSS transition on a 300ms delay after the panel opens. If they appear static, try clicking off the KPI FORECAST tab and back onto it. The animation only triggers once per tab open via setTimeout(animateBars, 100).
Is the AI output different every time I run the same query?
Yes. Groq llama-3.3-70b generates a fresh completion each time with default temperature settings. The PRELOADED output (visible before any AI call) is static and serves as a benchmark, but once you run a query or scenario, all subsequent output is live-generated.
