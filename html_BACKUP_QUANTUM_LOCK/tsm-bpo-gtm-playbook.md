## PART 1 — HEALTHCARE RCM DENIAL RECOVERY

### The Problem You're Solving
Medical practices, billing companies, and hospital systems collectively write off billions in denied claims every year — not because they can't be recovered, but because the recovery process is slow, manual, and inconsistently worked. The average denial rate is 5-10% of submitted claims. Recovery rates on worked denials average 60-70%. The gap is the opportunity.

Your war room processes a denial in under 60 seconds. A human billing specialist takes 20-45 minutes. That gap is your entire pitch.

---

### Your Offer (Healthcare)

**Name it:** TSM Denial Recovery Service

**The pitch in one sentence:**
"We work your denial backlog on contingency — 15% of recovered amount, zero cost if we don't collect."

**What you deliver:**
- Full denial analysis via 6-engine war room
- Written action report per denial (payer, reason code, appeal strategy, deadline)
- Drafted appeal letters ready to submit
- Monthly recovery summary with dollar amounts

**Who you target:**
- Independent medical practices (5-20 providers)
- Independent billing companies (they have 10-50 practice clients — one relationship = volume)
- Urgent care groups
- Specialty practices (ortho, cardiology, oncology — high dollar denials)

---

### Week-by-Week Action Plan

**Week 1 — Set up your outreach infrastructure**

1. Create a simple one-page PDF offer document (problem, solution, pricing, contact)
2. Set up a Google Form or Typeform as your intake — clients submit: practice name, EHR system, average monthly denial volume, top denial reasons
3. Create a LinkedIn company page for TSM if you don't have one
4. Identify 50 target contacts on LinkedIn: search "medical billing manager," "RCM director," "practice administrator," "billing coordinator" — filter to your state first, then expand
5. Connect with 10-15 per day with a personalized note (see template below)

**Week 2 — Outreach blitz**

1. Send first-touch LinkedIn messages to all 50 contacts
2. Post your first LinkedIn content piece (see screenshot strategy below)
3. Follow up with anyone who accepted your connection request
4. Identify 3-5 billing companies (not just practices) — they are the multiplier
5. Reach out to 2 medical practice Facebook groups or RCM LinkedIn groups and introduce yourself

**Week 3 — First free engagements**

1. Offer 3 practices a free 30-day denial recovery pilot
2. Run their denial documents through the war room
3. Deliver written reports within 24 hours of receiving documents
4. Document everything — recovery amounts, turnaround time, denial reasons surfaced

**Week 4 — Convert and expand**

1. Present results to pilot clients — show the numbers
2. Convert at least 2 to paid contingency agreements
3. Ask each for a referral or testimonial
4. Begin outreach to billing companies using pilot results as proof

## PART 2 — CONSTRUCTION DISPUTE RECOVERY

### The Problem You're Solving
Construction is the most document-intensive industry in the world and one of the worst at managing document risk. Change orders get disputed. Retainage gets withheld. Subcontractors file liens. Pay applications get rejected. Most GCs and subcontractors manage this with spreadsheets, email threads, and phone calls to attorneys — slow, expensive, and reactive.

Your war room analyzes a construction document in under 60 seconds across 6 dimensions: document intelligence, field ops, financial impact, legal exposure, compliance, and executive dispatch. That's a $500/hour construction attorney compressed into a 60-second analysis.

---

### Your Offer (Construction)

**Name it:** TSM Construction Document Intelligence Service

**The pitch in one sentence:**
"We analyze your change orders, lien notices, and pay application disputes — surfacing financial exposure, legal deadlines, and next steps — within 24 hours of receipt."

**Pricing options:**
- Per-document: $75-150 per document depending on complexity
- Monthly retainer: $500-1,500/month for up to 20 documents
- Contingency on dispute recovery: 10% of recovered amount on disputed change orders

**Who you target:**
- Subcontractors (they get the worst end of payment disputes — most motivated buyers)
- Small to mid-size GCs (5-50 employees — large enough to have document volume, small enough to not have in-house legal)
- Construction law firms (white-label — they use your analysis as a first-pass triage tool)
- Construction project managers and owners reps

---

### Week-by-Week Action Plan

**Week 1 — Set up**

1. Create your one-page offer PDF for construction
2. Identify 50 LinkedIn targets: search "construction project manager," "subcontractor," "general contractor," "construction owner," "construction attorney" — filter to your metro area first
3. Join 2-3 construction LinkedIn groups and AGC (Associated General Contractors) local chapter if available
4. Set up intake form: project name, document type, document upload, urgency level

**Week 2 — Outreach**

1. Send connection requests to all 50 targets with personalized note
2. Post first LinkedIn construction content piece
3. Reach out directly to 5 construction law firms — offer to provide AI-assisted document triage as a value-add for their clients
4. Look for local subcontractor associations — they often have newsletters and member directories

**Week 3 — Free pilots**

1. Offer 3 subcontractors or GCs a free document analysis on one active dispute
2. Run the documents, deliver the report within 24 hours
3. Include: risk score, financial exposure, legal deadlines, recommended next steps, specific apps to use

**Week 4 — Convert**

1. Present results — show them what a $75 analysis would have cost vs. what they paid their attorney for the same information
2. Offer a monthly retainer for ongoing document monitoring
3. Ask for referrals to their subcontractor or GC network

## PART 4 — THE FIRST 3 CLIENTS SCRIPT (Phone/Zoom)

When you get on a call, use this structure:

1. **Open with their problem** — "Tell me about your current denial situation / your change order dispute process." Listen for 5 minutes. Don't pitch yet.

2. **Reflect the pain** — "So you're basically manually researching each one, and the ones older than 90 days are just getting written off?" Let them confirm.

3. **Show the contrast** — "Can I show you something? I'm going to paste one of your document types into our system and show you what comes out in 60 seconds." Screen share the war room live.

4. **Run it live** — Use a sample document if they don't have one ready. Show the engines firing. Show the output. Show the mission steps.

5. **Close simple** — "We'll do your first batch for free. If you like what you see, we work on contingency — 15% of what we recover for healthcare, flat fee per document for construction. Zero risk to try."
