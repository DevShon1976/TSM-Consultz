01 · Overview

What Is the War Room Suite?

The TSM War Room Suite is a multi-stage AI-powered workflow that takes a document — a denied claim, an invoice dispute, a legal matter — and walks your team through every step to resolve it, with AI generating the analysis, routing, and action plan automatically.

💡
New to TSM? Start with the New EU Checklist at the bottom of this guide. It covers exactly what to do on your first case in under 10 minutes.
✦
The TSM Advantage A traditional BPO queue takes 3–7 business days and requires manual handoffs between teams. TSM runs the same analysis in minutes, generates a step-by-step repair plan, and escalates with full context pre-loaded — no re-entry, no dropped context.

03 · Stage 1

Document Intake — The War Room

The War Room is your starting point for every case. You select the document type, paste or upload the document text, and tell the system what it's dealing with before any AI runs.

1
Select the Document Type
Required First ▶
  • 📋
    Click the correct document type button
    Choose from the buttons at the top of the War Room (e.g. Claims Report, Denial Letter, DME Claim, AP Aging, ERA Batch). This tells the 6-engine pipeline what kind of analysis to run. Selecting the wrong type will produce mismatched results.
    Example: For a Medicare DME denial → select "DME Claim"
  • ℹ
    Available document types vary by vertical
    Insurance: Claims Report, Denial Letter, DME Claim, AP Aging, ERA Batch, Underwriting, CE Renewal. Healthcare: same plus clinical notes. FinOps: Invoice, Variance Report. Construction: Defect Report, RFI. Legal: Contract, Filing.
2
Paste the Document Text
Required ▶
  • 📝
    Paste into the large text box labeled "Paste Document Text"
    Copy the full text of the document — EOB, denial letter, invoice, contract — and paste it directly into the box. Include all fields: patient name, dates, codes, amounts, payer, and denial reason. The more complete the paste, the better the engine output.
    Min 50 characters. Include denial codes like CO-5, CO-29 if present.
  • ⚠
    Do not summarize — paste the full raw text
    The engines are designed to extract structure from raw document text. Summarizing or paraphrasing before pasting degrades AI accuracy. Paste exactly what the document says, including headers and line items.
3
Set the Client Mission (Optional but Recommended)
Enhances AI ▶
  • 🎯
    Type a mission context in the Mission field
    This is a short description of the goal — what the client needs resolved. Example: "Appeal CO-5 denial for DME Oxygen Concentrator. Patient is Medicare Beneficiary. $3,200 at risk. Need prior auth documentation." This context flows into every engine and improves the quality of all AI outputs.
    Look for the "SET CLIENT MISSION" input in the right panel or top of the War Room.
04 · Stage 2

Run the 6-Engine Analysis Pipeline

The engine pipeline is the core of the War Room. Six specialized AI engines analyze your document in sequence, each building on the last, to produce a comprehensive analysis and recommended actions.

💡
One Click Does Everything Press the FIRE ALL 6 ENGINES button. All six engines run sequentially. Wait for the status to show "DONE" for each engine before escalating. The full run typically takes 30–90 seconds depending on document length.
E1
Engine 01 — Claim Intelligence
Always First ▶
  • 🔍
    What it does
    Extracts core document fields: claim/policy ID, total amount, key parties, document type, and denial codes. This is the foundation all other engines build on. If E1 misreads a field, verify your document paste is complete and rerun.
  • 👁
    What to check in the output
    Confirm DOC TYPE, CLAIM/POLICY ID, TOTAL AMOUNT, and KEY PARTIES are correctly extracted. If any field shows "Not found," your document text may be incomplete.
    Output appears in the ENGINE OUTPUTS panel on the right side
E2
Engine 02 — Risk & Financial Exposure
Financial Analysis ▶
  • 📊
    What it does
    Calculates a Risk Score (0–100) and Financial Exposure. Higher scores indicate greater urgency. A score above 70 typically means CO-29 timely filing risk or high-dollar claim. The exposure figure tells you how much revenue is at stake.
  • ⚑
    Risk Score above 80 = escalate today
    Any case scoring 80+ should be escalated to the Strategist and the Executive Portal the same day. Note the appeal deadline shown in the output and set a calendar reminder if applicable.
    Risk 80+ triggers automatic [URGENT] flags in the Mission Guide steps
E3
Engine 03 — Revenue Opportunity
Recovery Analysis ▶
  • 💰
    What it does
    Identifies potential recoverable revenue and specific recovery actions. Shows what can be appealed, corrected, or re-billed. This output directly feeds the Strategist's N3 BNCA Synthesis node to generate the recovery strategy.
E4
Engines 04–05 — Compliance & Pattern
Compliance + Trends ▶
  • ⚖
    Compliance Check (E4)
    Flags any regulatory or payer-policy violations detected in the document. For insurance: checks NCCI edits, modifier compliance, ABN requirements, and COB rules. For legal: checks filing deadlines and required clauses.
  • 📈
    Pattern Analysis (E5)
    Identifies whether this denial or issue matches known patterns — repeat payer behavior, systemic billing errors, or recurring defect types. Use this to flag whether your organization needs a process fix, not just a case fix.
E6
Engine 06 — BNCA App Dispatcher
Most Important ▶
  • 🚀
    What it does — and why it matters most
    Engine 06 is the decision engine. It reads all five prior engine outputs, scores every relevant TSM app against your specific case, then generates: (1) a ranked list of the best apps to use, (2) an executive AI narrative summarizing the entire case, and (3) step-by-step instructions for each recommended app tailored to your document.
    The "ESCALATE TO STRATEGIST" button appears after E6 completes
  • ⚠
    Read the E6 output before escalating
    E6 tells you which apps to open and what to do in them, specific to your case. This is the most actionable output in the War Room. If it says "open Coding Validator and apply Modifier 59," that is your next step — not general advice.
05 · Stage 3

The Mission Guide Panel

The Mission Guide is the floating panel in the bottom-right corner of every War Room. It activates automatically after the engine pipeline completes and gives you a personalized, step-by-step repair workflow for your specific anomaly.

⚡ Mission Guide = Your Personal Case Playbook

Every time the engines finish, the Mission Guide reads the results and generates a custom 5-step action plan specific to your denial code, document type, and payer. It also shows which apps to use, how TSM resolves the issue versus how legacy BPO would handle it, and pre-written AI prompts you can fire directly into the query box.

A
Reading the Mission Guide
▶
  • 🔴
    Anomaly Detected badge + Denial Code tag
    At the top of the panel you'll see the anomaly type (e.g. "CO-5 Denial — DME Claim") and an orange denial code tag. For insurance and healthcare cases, you'll also see appeal routing chips: urgency level, the team responsible, and the exact appeal path to follow.
  • 📊
    Mission Progress bar
    Tracks how many of the 5 steps you've completed. Each time you mark a step done (click the "Mark done ✓" button on a step), the bar advances. At 5/5, the case is ready for escalation.
  • ◈
    Apps Used in This Workflow chips
    Shows the 2–4 specific TSM apps the AI recommends for your case. These are not generic — they are selected based on the denial code and document type. Open each one from your nav bar and follow the instructions in Step 3 of the execution steps.
  • ⚡
    TSM Way vs Old BPO Way comparison
    Shows the time and process difference between resolving the case in TSM versus a legacy BPO queue. Use this context when briefing supervisors or clients on case status.
B
Working Through the 5 Execution Steps
▶
  • 1️⃣
    The active step is highlighted in cyan
    Always work the currently-active (cyan-numbered) step first. Do not skip steps — each builds on the previous. Steps marked [URGENT] in orange have time-sensitive deadlines and should be addressed before anything else.
    ⚡ URGENT steps usually involve timely filing deadlines (CO-29) or high-risk authorization gaps
  • 📝
    Read the instruction and field hint on each step
    Each step has a title (what to do), an instruction (how to do it, with specific field names and actions), and a field hint in cyan italic (the exact field, app, or button to interact with). Follow all three before marking done.
  • ✓
    Click "Mark done ✓" to advance
    Once you've completed a step's action in the relevant app, click the "Mark done ✓" button. The step turns green with a checkmark, the next step activates, and the progress bar advances. Your progress is saved automatically.
  • ↻
    Use Regenerate if the steps don't fit your case
    If the generated steps feel too generic or miss the specifics of your case, click the ↻ Regenerate button. The AI will re-analyze the stored mission context and produce a fresh set of steps. This also works after you add new information to the War Room.
C
Using the Ready-to-Use Prompts
▶
  • ✈
    Click a prompt button to fire it into the AI query box
    The two ✈ prompt buttons at the bottom of the panel are pre-written queries tailored to your specific denial code and case. Clicking one automatically pastes the text into the AI query box on the page — you just click "Send" or your query button to run it.
    If the auto-paste doesn't work, the prompt is also copied to your clipboard
06 · Stage 4

The Strategist

The Strategist is the analytical layer between the War Room and Executive Portal. It runs 4 specialized AI nodes (N1–N4) that synthesize your War Room data into a full case strategy, recovery plan, and escalation recommendation.

S1
How to Arrive at the Strategist
▶
  • →
    Use the "Escalate →" button in the Mission Guide panel
    Once all 5 Mission Guide steps are complete (or you've confirmed urgency requires immediate escalation), click the Escalate → button at the bottom of the Mission Guide. This transfers the full session payload — all engine outputs, the anomaly type, payer, amount, and denial context — directly to the Strategist. No re-entry required.
    Alternatively: click STRATEGIST in the top navigation bar
  • ⚠
    Do not navigate directly to the Strategist without War Room data
    The Strategist reads session data set by the War Room engines. If you open it directly without completing the War Room pipeline, it will show "Waiting for war room data..." in all nodes and produce no output. Always run the War Room first.
S2
The 4 Strategist Nodes — What Each Does
▶
  • N1
    N1 — Situation Assessment
    Synthesizes the claim landscape, denial patterns, exposure summary, and risk posture into a structured situation brief. This is the "what is happening and how serious is it" layer. Read this first to confirm the AI understood your case correctly.
  • N2
    N2 — Strategic Analysis
    Generates 2–3 strategic options for resolving the case, with trade-offs, resource requirements, and a recommended timeline for each. Use this to choose your resolution path before running N3.
  • N3
    N3 — BNCA Synthesis (Most Actionable)
    Produces the BNCA (Best Next Corrective Action): a specific, ranked list of actions with assignments, deadlines, and expected recovery amounts. This is what you act on. Copy the BNCA output and share it with your billing or operations team as the work order.
    BNCA output includes: action, assigned team, deadline, expected outcome
  • N4
    N4 — Executive Recommendation
    Generates the final recommendation for leadership: recovery probability, whether principal escalation is needed, and a one-paragraph executive summary. This output pre-populates the Executive Portal.
S3
Assign to Teams from the Strategist
▶
  • 👥
    Use the "Assign To →" row at the bottom of each Strategist output
    After each node completes, you'll see assignment buttons: BILLING COORD, OFFICE MGR, RCM MANAGER (insurance/HC) or the equivalent for your vertical. Click the button to route the output to that team's queue. The receiving team sees the full node output in their dashboard.
  • 🔄
    Use Re-Run to refresh a node with updated context
    If you've updated the War Room data after running the Strategist, you can click RE-RUN on any individual node to regenerate just that node. You don't need to re-run all 4.
07 · Stage 5

The Executive Portal

The Executive Portal is the final stage of every case. It presents the full case in a leadership-ready format: financial exposure, recovery probability, strategic recommendation, and a sign-off flow for resolution or escalation to principal review.

X
What the Executive Portal Displays
▶
  • 📊
    Case summary card with financials
    Shows the case ID, document type, payer, amount at risk, risk score, recovery probability, and appeal deadline in a single card. This is the one-screen brief a supervisor or executive needs to make a decision.
  • ✅
    Approve, Escalate to Principal, or Close
    Three resolution options are available: Approve for Action (team handles per BNCA), Escalate to Principal (senior review required — use when N4 recommends principal escalation), and Close Case (issue resolved or not recoverable). Each option logs a timestamped entry.
    Always read the N4 recommendation before choosing an option
  • 📄
    Export case record
    Use the Export button to download the full case record as a structured document. Include this with any appeal submissions or in your audit trail. The export includes all engine outputs, the BNCA, and the executive recommendation.

09 · Reference

Denial Code Quick Reference

Insurance and Healthcare war rooms auto-detect these codes from your document. When detected, the Mission Guide automatically routes you to the correct appeal team and pre-loads the missing documents into Step 1.

Code Denial Reason Missing Docs Required Appeal Team Urgency
CO-5 Procedure inconsistent with place of service CMN, W-9, Prior Authorization Authorization & Billing Team HIGH
CO-4 Service inconsistent with covered benefit Medical necessity letter, Physician order Clinical Appeals Team MEDIUM
CO-16 Claim lacks required information Missing modifier, NPI/taxonomy correction Billing Corrections Team LOW
CO-22 Covered by another payer per COB COB form, Secondary payer EOB, Insurance verify COB/Eligibility Team MEDIUM
CO-97 Bundled service — unbundling required Modifier 59 documentation, Operative report Coding & Compliance Team MEDIUM
PR-96 Non-covered charge — patient responsibility ABN signed form, Patient consent Patient Financial Services HIGH
CO-50 Not deemed medically necessary Medical necessity documentation, Physician letter Clinical Appeals Team HIGH
CO-29 Timely filing limit expired Proof of timely filing, EOB from primary Appeals & Escalations Team HIGH — Act same day
⚠
CO-29 Is Always Same-Day Priority Timely filing denials have a hard deadline: typically 30 days from the denial date to submit proof of filing. If CO-29 appears in any case, treat it as the highest priority item regardless of dollar amount.
